Future Well Ageing

A community-based program for prevention and interventions using welfare technology for a healthy everyday life

Project Summary

This project aims to develop innovative models utilizing welfare technology to enhance the well-being and participation of older adults in everyday activities, ultimately reducing the dependency on primary and homecare services. By addressing the needs of older adults at risk or with frailty, along with their caregivers and staff, the project seeks to implement effective interventions using digital technologies.

Through five interconnected projects, this interdisciplinary program will employ qualitative and quantitative methods to explore the efficacy of technology-based interventions in various contexts. These projects will investigate key research questions, including the impact of prevention programs on frailty, the integration of technology into primary healthcare, and the enhancement of homecare services through digital solutions.

The findings of this research will have significant implications for addressing the growing challenges faced by older adults in Europe, where frailty affects a substantial portion of the population, leading to increased costs in care provision. By leveraging digital solutions, this project aims to empower older adults to maintain their independence, while also providing support for caregivers and staff in delivering more efficient and tailored care.

Collaboration between regional stakeholders, including healthcare providers and academic institutions, will ensure the relevance and practicality of the interventions developed. By engaging older adults and their caregivers in the research process, this project will not only generate valuable insights but also foster a sense of ownership and empowerment among the target population. Ultimately, this project seeks to contribute to a paradigm shift in elderly care, where technology plays a central role in promoting well-being and independence in older age.
