Management of the Program

Maksims Kornevs
10 April, 2024

This program leader PL (Guidetti) will plan and set up regular meetings with the project investigators (PI) (Asaba, Bergström, Emami, Meijer, Patomella) as a management group (MG) of the program that will meet every second months to discuss plans and progress during the first three years. This MG group will also be responsible for external contacts, creating a data management plan, ethical review, managing the budget and reporting to FORTE. KTH (with Professor Meijer) will create the Healty Aging into the Future web platform including all projects to be used as a database for presenting the different projects, sharing results and outcomes from the program.

The MG will also invite participants representing different stakeholder groups as older adults, significant others, informal carer/care partners and staff members to act as a reference group for the program. There are also parts within the different projects where co-creation methods will be used to incorporate the end-users’ voices and views to be able to create the best technical solutions within prevention and interventions needed for Good quality, local health care.

The members in each project will have operative meetings around organisation of the work for the specific project and plan for the next step. All PhD candidates and other researchers in the project will in addition be invited to a regular seminar series at the department of NVS, focusing on presentations of manuscript drafts and theoretical discussions. Each semester a workshop will be initiated for all researchers who work actively in the program to enable the sharing of knowledge by presenting findings from the different projects, discuss the progress and drawbacks, invite to new research ideas for the future. These workshops will enable cross-project sharing and allow for formatively identifying potential parts that need additional support or strategies that can serve as springboard for growth and accumulation of knowledge. This will be the arena for developments of shared knowledge, theory, and new methodology. The work in each project will be action oriented and further meetings will be held when needed. All projects will be initiated the first year 2023 and last for 6 years.
