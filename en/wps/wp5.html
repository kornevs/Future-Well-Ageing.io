A systems modelling approach to implementation of an intervention for older adults

IThe objective of this project is to characterise systems variables (elements), and their relationships (connections), relevant to different intervention within prevention, reablement approach and the homecare, homecare user needs and satisfaction. This to examine the effects of change-work and interventions, such as the person-centred prevention and reablement approach, on the delivery of the services, workload, work-related stress, homecare user experience and other organisational factors. By evaluating and synthesising the implementation and outcomes from project A, B, C and D to create an implementation model to be used in the different future primary and homecare in region and municipality settings.

The research question addressed in project E is: What are the components and mechanisms of impact in the different services? What could facilitate the process of change within the health care system and the context?

Study design and research setting

This study is of a synthesizing nature and follows therefore a modelling methodology rather than an intervention study design. The study uses participatory model building of system dynamics models as used 48 to capture the diverse perspectives of different stakeholders, as well as the expertise of the researchers who conducted studies A – D.

This project will be focusing on Swedish homecare and tax-funded universal health systems.

As a methodological innovation, the group around Meijer at KTH has developed a step-wise modelling approach, where the following steps are followed:

  1. Literature review of relevant literature of the domain.
  2. A scan of all literature found for causal logic and effect chains.
  3. Construction of a first System Dynamics model, with literature validity (only on structural aspects).
  4. Model review using a participatory approach in which different stakeholder groups get to improve, validate, or criticise the model.
  5. A researcher validation to see whether their findings still hold valid in the improved model.
  6. A scenario testing phase to validate the model for its conclusions / use with professionals.
